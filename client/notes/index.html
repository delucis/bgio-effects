Initial render

Effects are not emitted the first time the client gets state from boardgame.io.

You should think of effects as the ‚Äúglue‚Äù between two states: the transition from state 1 to state 2 produces effects. For this reason, effects are not emitted during the initial render, where only one state is present.

An example

A game board includes a die that is rolled on certain moves. The state before and after rolling the die might look like:

  1. Die shows 4 (before move)
  2. Die shows 6 (after move)

To trigger the animation each time the die is rolled, we can add an effect to the move so that between state 1 and state 2, a ‚Äúroll die‚Äù effect fires and animates the transition from showing 4 to showing 6.

Now let‚Äôs say a player closes their game and comes back later. Nothing more has happened and state 2 is still the most recent:

  1. Die shows 6

Timing precision

This library is not designed with highly precise timing and animation synchronisation in mind. Effects are emitted from a requestAnimationFrame callback and the general implementation aims to be as performant and simple as possible. Exact timing will depend on the frame rate of a user‚Äôs browser and the accuracy of performance.now() (which may be limited for security reasons).
